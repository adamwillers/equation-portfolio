// About

From My Own Channel
To Editing For Others

I started editing because I was running my own gaming YouTube channel — Minecraft builds, Skyrim playthroughs, late-night horror sessions. I taught myself DaVinci Resolve because I wanted my edits to actually feel the way the game felt when I was playing it.

Three years later, that channel is still mine — but most of my time now goes to editing for other creators. Stream highlights, montages, vlogs, podcasts, TikToks and Reels across games like Minecraft, Buckshot Roulette, Roblox, Fallout 4, Skyrim, and a long list of indie horror titles.

What I bring isn't just technical skill. It's that I actually understand the culture — what makes a Twitch clip hit, what a YouTube viewer bounces off of, why a 30-second Reel works. I've been on your side of the camera.

// Services

What I Do

Full-stack post-production for gaming and creator content — from a single 30-second clip to a fully edited 40-minute episode.

Zoom & Pan Effects

Smooth keyframe motion that adds energy to facecams, gameplay reactions, and talking-head segments without feeling jarring.

Subtitles & Captions

Styled captions that match your brand — animated, highlighted, and tuned for sound-off viewing on TikTok, Reels, and Shorts.

Color Correction & Grading

From raw OBS captures to cinematic looks — proper correction first, then a grade that fits your channel's vibe.

Motion Tracking

Stick a face, an effect, or a meme right where it needs to be — even when the source footage is shaky gameplay.

Visual Effects

Glows, particles, screen shakes, energy bursts — composited cleanly without the "effect pack" look everyone can spot.

Sound Design

Layered SFX, punchy whooshes, EQ'd dialogue, and music ducking that actually makes your voice sit right on top.

Camera Shake

Reactive shake that hits on the beat, the kill, or the jump-scare — never random, always purposeful.

Green Screen / Keying

Clean keys for webcam overlays, custom backgrounds, and PiP layouts — no halo, no spill, no YouTuber-in-2015 look.

Licensed Music Sync

Beat-matched cuts, licensed tracks, and proper audio mixing so your edit lands on the drop — not near it.
